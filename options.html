Most organizations only plan for the intern they imagine.
Not the three they'll actually get.

Your organization will receive interns from all three types. Each has a different profile, different knowledge base, and different role expectations. Understanding all three is what lets you design a system that actually works — for every intern you'll get.

⚡ Type A

Skilled

Technical Proficient

Comes in with technical knowledge and can start contributing to real work within the short period of time. Needs direction more than teaching.

🌱 Type B

Developing

Technical Novice

Has little foundational knowledge but needs structured mentorship to apply it. Highly coachable and motivated — best for teams willing to invest in long-term talent.

🔀 Type C

Skilled

Non-technical Proficient

Brings strong domain skills in areas like product management, IT admin, technical writing, or digital marketing. Tech-aware but not a developer.

🌿 Type D

Developing

Non-technical Novice

Interested in non‑technical roles but has little practical experience. Brings enthusiasm, reliability, and a strong desire to learn — ideal for teams ready to train from the ground up.
