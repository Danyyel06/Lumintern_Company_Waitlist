Student Category

TYPE D

Beginners in non‑technical roles who are willing to learn.

Who they are

Students who are interested in non‑technical roles like design, marketing, IT support, or business operations but have little to no practical experience. They may have done some basic coursework or simple tasks and are eager to learn in a supportive environment.

What they know

- Basic familiarity with common digital tools (Microsoft Office, Google Workspace, Canva, social media platforms)
- Awareness of core concepts in their area of interest (e.g., what a wireframe is, how a campaign runs, basic IT troubleshooting)
- Good soft skills: communication, reliability, teamwork, and a willingness to learn
- Ability to follow instructions and ask questions when unsure

What they've done

- Completed introductory coursework, online tutorials, or workshops in their chosen field
- Assisted with simple tasks in student clubs or group projects (e.g., drafting social media posts, organizing files, basic data entry)
- Created a few basic outputs (a simple flyer, a short content draft, a basic spreadsheet)
- Helped peers with simple tech issues or coordinated small events

What you provide

- Clear, entry‑level tasks with well‑defined expectations
- Hands‑on training and regular constructive feedback
- Opportunities to shadow experienced team members
- A supportive environment to build confidence and foundational skills

Suggested Roles

Design Assistant (Entry) Social Media Assistant (Entry) IT Support Trainee Administrative Assistant Content Assistant (Entry) User Research Assistant (Entry) Junior Data Entry Clerk Customer Support Trainee Marketing Assistant (Entry) Office Operations Trainee
