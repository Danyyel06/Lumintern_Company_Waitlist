Student Category

TYPE C

Students who prefer non‑coding roles in tech environments

Who they are

Students who choose to focus on non‑technical or less technical aspects of technology. They may have foundational coding exposure but prefer paths like design, marketing, IT support, or business operations. They are interested in working in tech without heavy programming demands.

What they know

- Familiar with basic digital tools (Google Workspace, Microsoft Office)
- Awareness of technology concepts (how websites/apps work)
- Strong communication, organisation, teamwork
- Willingness to learn domain‑specific tools on the job
- Interest in non‑coding tech roles (design, marketing, IT support, admin)

What they've done

- Created content (posts, flyers, simple graphics) for a student club
- Assisted with event coordination or administrative tasks
- Wrote clear instructions or helped document a project
- Helped peers troubleshoot basic tech issues
- Received positive feedback on reliability and communication

What you provide

- Clear guidance and entry‑level tasks that build confidence
- Opportunities to work alongside technical and business teams
- Mentorship to develop domain expertise and soft skills
- Exposure to real‑world workflows and tools

Suggested Roles

UI/UX Design Assistant Digital Marketing Assistant Junior Product Coordinator Technical Writer (Entry) Content Assistant User Research Assistant Social Media Assistant Business Operations Assistant IT Help Desk Assistant IT Support Assistan
